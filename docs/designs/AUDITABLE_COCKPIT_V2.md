## Why This Plan Exists

Landscape research (2026-05-29) found the "private, local, in-browser CSV finance
analyzer" niche is already crowded with free tools: Personal Finance Visualizer,
Financial Expense Analyzer (Chase/Revolut/Wise/Monzo), Discover Statement Analyzer,
Finsight. "Local + private + CSV" is now table stakes, not a moat.

The real, unclaimed edge: **accountant-grade auditability + founder-grade runway/forecast
credibility in one coherent cockpit.** Layer-2 competitors are single-purpose categorizers
or toy single-number calculators. Full accounting suites (Wave, Quicken, Zoho) are heavy,
cloud-locked, account-required. Nobody delivers defensible, traceable cash truth with no
account and no upload.

Freelancer pain research confirms the wedge: 88% of spreadsheets contain errors, no audit
trail, distrust of the numbers. Our "show the formula, prove the math, trace every figure"
stance is the direct answer to spreadsheet distrust.

## Vision

### 10x Check
The financial cockpit that lets a solo operator walk into any conversation (accountant,
investor, tax, spouse) with numbers they can defend line by line, derived live from their
own files, that they understand because every number shows its work. TurboTax-level
confidence for cash truth, with no account, no upload, no AI black box.

### Platonic Ideal
Founder drops in a bank export. In 3 seconds: "7.2 months of runway. Here's exactly why."
Every KPI is clickable and expands to the formula, the exact rows that fed it, and one
plain-English sentence. When something is off (duplicate, transfer counted as income,
one-time spike), the cockpit flags it BEFORE the number lands, so they never present a
wrong figure. Then they hand the whole thing to their accountant as a clean workbook the
accountant can trace. The feeling: defensible calm.

## Accepted Scope (this cycle)

- **Positioning flip.** Rewrite README, roadmap "Positioning" section, and in-app
  privacy/value copy to lead with "auditable cash truth you and your accountant can both
  trust." Privacy becomes a supporting feature, not the headline. No code-logic change.
- **Show-the-math auditability layer.** Every cockpit KPI (revenue, outflow, net cash,
  burn, runway) becomes clickable and reveals: the formula, the exact imported rows that
  fed it, and one plain-English sentence. Surfaces math that already exists in `src/finance`.
- **Anomaly pre-flight.** Before KPIs render, surface "we found N things that might distort
  your numbers" (suspected duplicates, transfers counted as income, one-time spikes), each
  with a one-click include/exclude toggle that live-updates the KPIs. Builds on the
  duplicate/transfer/rejected-row detection already referenced in `cockpit-kpis.ts`.
- **Accountant handoff workbook export.** Single multi-sheet export: summary KPIs with their
  formulas, full categorized ledger, excluded/rejected rows with reasons, detected
  duplicates/transfers. Accountant can verify every number the cockpit showed.

## NOT in Scope (explicit decisions — 2026-05-29)

Guiding principle the owner set: **be good at one thing and be damn good at it.** The
dashboard (showing defensible cash truth) is the core product. Anything that turns it into
a different product is out.

- **Admin panel — OUT.** Raised by the owner's boss from an office-operations angle
  (inventory stock, present/absent tracking). Helpful instinct, but it's a different product
  with accounts/server, and the dashboard is the core. Rejected to preserve focus and the
  local-first premise.
- **Invoice creation / sending — OUT.** Breaks the privacy hard-rule (implies storing and
  transmitting client data) and duplicates tools freelancers already use (Payoneer, Wise),
  which already keep invoice history. Reinventing a solved problem, worse. Rejected.
- **Full Invoices/Receivables module — OUT** for V2 (roadmap had it as a "later module";
  now explicitly deprioritized given the above).
- Server-side transaction storage, bank integrations, AI-as-hero (hard no per roadmap).
- Multi-user / hosted SaaS backend — out; this stays a public local-first utility.
